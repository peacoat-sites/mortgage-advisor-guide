Private mortgage insurance runs you somewhere between $50 and $200 a month for most borrowers. The good news: you can request removal once your loan-to-value ratio drops to 80%, and federal law forces your lender to kill it automatically at 78%. That's the Homeowners Protection Act doing its job.

## How PMI Removal Works

| Trigger | LTV Threshold | Action Required | Timeline | Cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Request Removal | 80% LTV | Written cancellation request to servicer | Servicer responds within 30 days | Appraisal $300-$600 (if needed) |
| Automatic Removal | 78% LTV | None-lender must cancel | Based on original amortization schedule | No cost |
| FHA (10%+ down) | N/A | Refinance to conventional | After 11 years or upon refinance | Refinance costs apply |
| FHA (<10% down) | N/A | Refinance to conventional | No automatic removal | Refinance costs apply |

**At 80% LTV:** Write to your lender requesting cancellation. They'll probably ask for a home appraisal (you pay for it, usually $300-$600) to verify your home's current value.

**At 78% LTV:** Your lender has to cancel PMI automatically. No letter required. No negotiation. It's the law under the HPA, based on your original amortization schedule regardless of what happened to the housing market.

**FHA loans work differently:** FHA mortgage insurance premium hangs around for life if your down payment was under 10%. Put down 10% or more and it drops off after 11 years. Want to escape it sooner? Refinance into a conventional loan, because that's basically your only option.

## Ways to Reach 80% LTV Faster

Make extra principal payments. Even $100 or $200 a month compounds quickly and shaves months or years off the timeline.

Home improvements that boost your appraised value lower your LTV directly. A new kitchen or bathroom renovation can do real work here.

If your neighborhood's appreciating, you might hit that 80% threshold faster than your standard payment schedule would suggest. Request a new appraisal to prove it.

A lump-sum paydown (bonus, tax refund, inheritance) applied straight to principal accelerates everything.

## When to Request PMI Removal

Once you think you've crossed 80% LTV, send a written cancellation request to your servicer. It needs to include your loan account number, a statement that you're requesting removal under the Homeowners Protection Act, and evidence of current value if you're betting on appreciation (which usually means ordering an appraisal).

Make a copy for yourself. Your servicer has 30 days to respond.
