Closing costs catch most buyers off guard. On a $400,000 purchase, you're looking at $8,000–$20,000 depending on your state, loan type, and what your specific lender decides to charge. The calculator below breaks it down line by line.

## What's Included in Closing Costs

**Lender fees**
- **Origination fee**, typically 0.5–1% of the loan amount. This covers underwriting, processing, and administration.
- **Appraisal**, $400–$700 for a standard single-family home. Most lenders require it.
- **Prepaid interest**, which runs from your closing date through the end of that month. Close on the 5th instead of the 25th and you'll prepay more interest.

**Title and escrow fees**
- **Title search**, confirms the seller actually owns the property. Usually $200–$400.
- **Lender's title insurance**, protects the lender if something goes wrong with ownership. Required. About 0.5% of loan amount.
- **Owner's title insurance**, optional but I'd recommend it anyway. About 0.3% of purchase price.
- **Escrow/settlement fee**, paid to the title company or attorney closing the deal. $800–$1,200 is typical.

**Government fees**
- **Recording fees**, charged by the county to file the deed and mortgage. $100–$400.
- **Transfer taxes**, and this one varies wildly. Delaware charges 2%. Most western states charge nothing. This is where your location makes the biggest difference.

**Prepaid escrow items**
- **Property tax reserve**, usually 3–6 months of property taxes held in escrow.
- **Homeowner's insurance**, lenders require 12–14 months prepaid at closing.

## Loan-Type Differences

**FHA loans** tack on an upfront mortgage insurance premium (MIP) of 1.75% of the loan amount. On a $350,000 loan that's $6,125, and you can roll it into the loan itself.

**VA loans** add a funding fee of 1.25–3.3% depending on your down payment and whether this is your first VA loan. First-time buyers putting down less than 5% pay 2.15%. Disabled veterans don't pay it at all.

**Conventional loans** skip government fees but usually require PMI if you're putting down less than 20%.

## Can You Negotiate Closing Costs?

You can on several of them.
- **Lender fees**, get Loan Estimates from multiple lenders. The origination fee bounces around.
- **Title insurance**, you can pick your own title company in most states. Shop around.
- **Settlement/escrow fee**, also shoppable.

Government recording fees, transfer taxes, and prepaid escrow amounts? Those are locked in.

**Seller concessions** work too. In a buyer's market, sellers will sometimes cover 2–6% of your closing costs as part of negotiations.
