Closing costs catch many buyers off guard. On a $400,000 purchase, expect to budget $8,000–$20,000 depending on your state, loan type, and the fees your specific lender charges. Use the estimator below for a detailed, itemized view.

## What's Included in Closing Costs

**Lender fees**
- **Origination fee**, typically 0.5–1% of the loan amount. Covers underwriting, processing, and administration.
- **Appraisal**, $400–$700 for a standard single-family home. Required by most lenders.
- **Prepaid interest**, interest from your closing date to the end of the month. Closing earlier in the month means more days of prepaid interest.

**Title and escrow fees**
- **Title search**, confirms the seller has clear ownership. Usually $200–$400.
- **Lender's title insurance**, protects the lender against title defects. Required. About 0.5% of loan amount.
- **Owner's title insurance**, optional but strongly recommended. About 0.3% of purchase price.
- **Escrow/settlement fee**, paid to the title company or attorney managing the closing. $800–$1,200 typically.

**Government fees**
- **Recording fees**, charged by the county to record the deed and mortgage. $100–$400.
- **Transfer taxes**, a significant variable. Delaware charges 2%, while many western states charge nothing. This is the biggest state-to-state difference in closing costs.

**Prepaid escrow items**
- **Property tax reserve**, typically 3–6 months of property taxes held in escrow.
- **Homeowner's insurance**, lenders require 12–14 months prepaid at closing.

## Loan-Type Differences

**FHA loans** add an upfront MIP of 1.75% of the loan amount, on a $350,000 loan that's $6,125, which can be rolled into the loan.

**VA loans** add a funding fee (1.25–3.3% depending on down payment and whether it's your first VA loan). First-time users putting down less than 5% pay 2.15%. Disabled veterans are exempt.

**Conventional loans** have no government fees but typically require PMI if down payment is under 20%.

## Can You Negotiate Closing Costs?

Yes, several fees are negotiable or can be shopped:
- **Lender fees**, compare Loan Estimates from multiple lenders. The origination fee varies widely.
- **Title insurance**, in most states you can choose your own title company. Get multiple quotes.
- **Settlement/escrow fee**, also shoppable.

Items you **cannot** negotiate: government recording fees, transfer taxes, and prepaid escrow amounts (set by your insurance/tax bills).

**Seller concessions** are another option. In a buyer's market, sellers sometimes agree to cover 2–6% of closing costs as part of the negotiation.
